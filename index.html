Overview

We fine-tune F5-TTS with LoRA adapters using a cross-lingual reconstruction objective: given a native Spanish (or Italian) transcript and its audio, the model is trained to reproduce the original speech. No accented English recordings are used at any point. At inference, supplying an English transcript to the same checkpoint yields English speech that carries the prosodic character of the training language — rhythm, stress, and intonation — without ever having heard Spanish-accented English.

The hypothesis is that prosodic priors are disentangled from phoneme identity within the model's internal representations, and that LoRA's low-rank constraint encourages the adapters to capture suprasegmental features rather than language-specific phonology.
